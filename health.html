SOCIALMEDIA ADDICTION

Social media addiction can significantly impact your health by increasing anxiety, depression, and feelings of loneliness. Excessive screen time can also lead to poor sleep quality, eye strain, and reduced physical activity. To protect your mental and physical health, it's essential to set boundaries on social media use, engage in offline activities, and prioritize face-to-face interactions with loved ones.

YeYintPhyo's Opinion

Social media addiction happens when people spend too much time on apps like Instagram, Facebook, or Twitter. This can make them feel like they always need to check their phones. It can cause problems like less sleep, less time with family and friends, and feeling anxious or depressed. It's important to take breaks and remember to enjoy real-life moments too.

Lu Khant Min's Opinion

Social media is when people use apps like Facebook or Instagram too much. It's a big problem for young people. It's good because they can talk to friends and learn things. But too much can make them sad or anxious. It's hard to stop because they like getting likes and comments. We need to teach them how to use it less and do other things instead. Parents and teachers need to help too.

Min Thant Htoo's Opinon

Social media addiction took over my life before I knew it. I spent hours scrolling, comparing myself to others, and seeking likes. It made me anxious and unhappy. I had to set limits and take breaks to feel better. Now, I enjoy real-life moments more and use social media with care. This balance helps me stay happy and present.
